Digital Intimacy

The beginning. Before the friction. When the screen felt like enough.

Act One is about the early language of a long-distance connection — the 3am messages, the timezone arithmetic, the way a phone notification can feel like a heartbeat. Five songs written in the space between two people who are trying to close a gap that geography keeps reopening.

II
In Development

Friction

The middle. Where love gets difficult. Where distance becomes more than miles.

Act Two is where the story changes tone. The sweetness of Act One gives way to something harder — misunderstandings that can't be fixed by a voice note, silences that last too long, the weight of loving someone whose past you can't fully see. These five songs live in the friction of a relationship that's real but difficult.

"Somebody Else Taught You Fear" might be the most important song in the trilogy. It's about loving someone whose damage predates you — the impossible task of trying to undo what someone else did.

"I Learned Your Language Wrong" is about the moment you realize you've been speaking the same words but meaning different things. Act Two doesn't have an easy ending.
